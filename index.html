home / bodies

This project uses a soundscape composition as a data prompt for interview-based research. A randomized sample of participants were asked to perform a soundscape composition specifically designed for at-home lockdown during COVID. At-home workers had a new challenge to deal with that distracted them from the flow of their labour: noise.  The great intruder, noise, was not necessarily loud. It was intrusive. It has introduced a jagged series of heterogenous time-scales, a consequence of the home operating in stacks of usually separate institutions: family on work on health on education. The soundscape was distributed to participants in order to make sense of and tune into the new soundscapes of interruption.  Participants were asked to perform the composition, found {here}, to produce sketches and drawings of the experiences, and to follow up with an interview to reflect on the lived concrete experience of living with and adjusting to the new at-home soundscapes.
